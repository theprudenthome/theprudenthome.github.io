About

The Prudent Home publishes practical writing on household money and self-sufficient living: subscription audits, grocery comparisons, garden returns, appliance energy math, and the quiet substitutions that stack up over a year.

What we publish

  • Cost audits. We add up what a specific category really costs a typical household, and where money leaks.
  • DIY vs. subscribe. When is making it yourself cheaper than paying for it? We do the arithmetic, measure where relevant, and call out time cost explicitly.
  • Garden and kitchen returns. What a modest garden actually yields in dollars, and what preserving pays back after accounting for jars, time, and failures.
  • Energy and utility math. Real power draws, real bills, real break-even points across common household appliances and services.

No-hype pledge

  • We do not give investment, tax, or legal advice. Anything that touches personal finance on this site is comparison and arithmetic, not guidance on markets.
  • Every number is labelled either tracked (we recorded it first-hand) or approximate (researched; verify current pricing before acting).
  • We name what did not work and where we were wrong. "What we tried that did not save money" is a section on most posts, not an afterthought.
  • No clickbait titles, no "make money fast", no astrology, no crypto or stock picks, no diet or medical claims.
  • No affiliate recommendation for a product we have not personally used or priced against named alternatives in the post.

How posts are produced

Drafts are written with help from AI tools against a strict template that will refuse to publish without cited figures, an honest limitations section, and real data backing any headline claim. The editorial review, the numbers in every table, and the final sign-off are human.

We publish a full disclosure of our affiliate relationships and a privacy policy, and we encourage readers to cross-check any number with the linked vendor or a professional before acting on it.

Who this is for

Working parents, students, first-time homeowners, renters, retirees — anyone who wants household money and home-economy writing that is concrete about numbers, honest about failures, and free of get-rich-quick nonsense.

Corrections and tips

If a figure looks wrong, or you want us to audit a category we have not yet covered, please reach out.
